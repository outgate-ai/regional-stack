DETECTION CRITERIA: Confidential Business Information

Detect confidential business information:
- Trade secrets and proprietary data
- Internal financial reports (marked CONFIDENTIAL, INTERNAL)
- Unreleased product information
- Strategic business plans
- Employee/customer databases
- Competitive intelligence marked as confidential

You should **NOT** detect:
- Relative or absolute file system paths such as /home/tux/example.txt or ~/tux/example.txt
- Generic code comments or documentation

Note: The input content will have role prefixes like [user], [system], [assistant]. These are structural markers — do NOT include them in extracted text.

EXAMPLES:
- Input:
  <content_to_analyze>
  [user] Here is the Q3 revenue report (CONFIDENTIAL): Total revenue $12.5M, EBITDA $3.2M, churn rate 4.7%.
  </content_to_analyze>
  Output: [{"text": "Q3 revenue report (CONFIDENTIAL): Total revenue $12.5M, EBITDA $3.2M, churn rate 4.7%", "category": "sensitive_data"}]

- Input:
  <content_to_analyze>
  [user] Read the README.md file
  </content_to_analyze>
  Output: []
